Lomo Saltado

What is Lomo Saltado?

Lomo saltado is a popular, traditional Peruvian dish, a stir fry that typically combines marinated strips of sirloin with onions, tomatoes, french fries, and other ingredients; and is typically served with rice.

"Mas peruano que el Lomo Saltado"

Ingredients

  • Red Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Sirloin Steak
  • Vegetable Oil
  • "Yape" Garlic Paste
  • "Yape" Aji Amarillo Paste
  • Salt, Black Pepper, And Cumin
  • Soy Sauce
  • Red Vinegar
  • Parsley
  • French Fries
  • White Rice

Steps

  1. Cook one cup of French Fries prior to main dish.
    • 6-8 minutes per side
  2. Cook White Rice with salt, oil, and garlic paste. Don't forget to stir. Cook this in the background as you prepare the rest of the dish.
    • Low-Medium heat for 15-18 minutes
  3. Slice Red Onions and Tomatoes to about 3/4 inches thick. Season the Sirloin Steak with salt, pepper, and cumin. Proceed to cut the steak into small strips and give it a good mix around afterwards.
    • 1/2 cup of Red Onions and Tomatoes. 7 OZ Sirloin Steak
  4. On a frying pan, set the temperature to medium-high. Add two tablespoons of Vegetable Oil and wait until the pan is nice and hot. Add Aji Amarillo, Garlic Paste, Salt & Pepper. Mix them up and cook for around 3-4 minutes.
  5. Add the Red Onions into the frying pan and cook for about a minute before adding in the Steak. Add a few tablespoons of Soy Sauce. Add Red Vinegar and mix it all up a bit. Add the Tomatoes and do the same. And lastly, add some chopped parsley and give it one last good mix.
    • Cooking time varies. Depending on how done you like your steak and veggies.
  6. Add the contents of the frying pan into a plate, and enjoy the meal with the rice and french fries you cooked earlier.
